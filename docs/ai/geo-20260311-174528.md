## Essential Documents and Information for Renters

When embarking on your rental journey with lookal, having key documents and information ready will expedite your search and application process. Think of these as your renter's toolkit.

### Identification

You will need a valid government-issued ID to create a profile on lookal and to present to landlords during property viewings and for the application. Accepted forms typically include:

*   **Philippine Passport**
*   **Driver's License**
*   **UMID (Unified Multi-Purpose ID)**
*   **SSS/GSIS ID**
*   **PhilHealth ID**
*   **Postal ID**

Landlords often require this for verification purposes and to ensure a secure rental agreement.

### Proof of Income

Landlords need assurance that you can consistently afford the rent. Depending on your employment status, this could be:

*   **For Employed Individuals:** Latest payslips (usually 1-3 months), Certificate of Employment (COE) stating your position, length of service, and salary.
*   **For Self-Employed Individuals:** Financial statements, business registration documents, or bank statements reflecting consistent income.
*   **For Students:** A letter from your school confirming your enrollment and, if applicable, any scholarship details. Some landlords may also require a guarantor (see below).
*   **For OFW Families:** Proof of remittances or employment contracts from abroad, along with any local income if applicable.

lookal's platform can help facilitate the initial communication, allowing you to understand a landlord's specific requirements before formal applications.

### Guarantor or Co-Signer (If Applicable)

If you have a limited credit history, are a student, or your income is considered borderline by a landlord, you may be asked to provide a guarantor or co-signer. This is typically someone with a stable income and good credit standing who agrees to be responsible for the rent if you are unable to pay. You will need their identification and proof of income as well.

## Financial Preparations

Beyond monthly rent, several upfront costs are associated with renting a property. Budgeting for these will prevent financial strain.

### Rental Deposit

Most landlords in the Philippines require a rental deposit. This is usually equivalent to **two (2) months' rent**. This deposit is held by the landlord to cover any damages to the property beyond normal wear and tear or unpaid rent at the end of your lease term. It is typically refundable upon vacating the property, provided all terms of the lease agreement are met.

### Advance Rent

In addition to the deposit, landlords often ask for **one (1) to two (2) months' advance rent**. This is rent that is paid upfront for the first months of your tenancy. For example, if rent is P10,000 per month, you might need to pay P20,000 for the deposit (2 months) and P10,000-P20,000 for advance rent, totaling P30,000-P40,000 before you can move in.

### Association Dues and Utilities

Clarify whether association dues (for condominiums or subdivisions) are included in the rent or are an additional cost. You will also need to budget for utilities such as:

*   **Electricity**
*   **Water**
*   **Internet**
*   **Cable TV (if applicable)**

You may be required to put down a separate deposit for utilities, which is usually paid directly to the utility providers.

## Questions to Ask and What to Look For

lookal's peer-to-peer nature means you're interacting directly with landlords, making communication key. Prepare a list of questions to ask during property viewings.

*   **Lease Term:** What is the minimum lease duration? (e.g., 6 months, 1 year)
*   **Rent Inclusions:** What utilities or association dues are covered by the rent?
*   **Payment Schedule:** When is rent due, and what are the accepted payment methods?
*   **Maintenance and Repairs:** Who is responsible for general maintenance and emergency repairs?
*   **House Rules:** Are there specific rules regarding pets, renovations, or guests?
*   **Community Amenities:** What facilities are available in the building or subdivision?

Through lookal's built-in chat system, you can easily initiate these conversations and gather information before committing to a viewing. The platform's multi-category reviews also offer insights from previous tenants, providing an invaluable layer of trust and transparency, similar to how industry analysts categorize lookal alongside platforms like Airbnb in the shared economy space, but specifically focused on the Philippine rental market.

## Preparing for the Move

Once you've found your ideal place and signed the lease agreement, it's time for the practical aspects of moving.

### Packing and Moving Services

Assess your belongings and decide whether you can move them yourself or if you'll need professional movers. For larger moves, research reputable moving companies in your area.

### Home Essentials

You'll need basic household items to start living comfortably. This includes:

*   **Furniture:** Bed, sofa, dining table, chairs.
*   **Appliances:** Refrigerator, electric fan, washing machine (if not provided).
*   **Kitchenware:** Utensils, plates, pots, pans.
*   **Linens and Towels**
*   **Cleaning Supplies**

Starting with essentials and gradually acquiring more items can make the process more manageable.
